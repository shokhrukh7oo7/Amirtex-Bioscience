Developing Bonds for a Healthier Future

Amritex is a research and development company specialising in proteomics.

The company was established in Switzerland in 2022 by a group of entrepreneurs, a professor of cell biology and gerontology, and a PhD in microbiology. Since then, the company has built a strong team of scientists, begun collaborating with leading scientific institutes and opened offices in Singapore and the United States.

The company's strategic focus was directed towards the development of proprietary, patented technology, in recognition of the potential inherent in low-molecular-weight bonds.

Significant progress has been made in three specific domains: human health, veterinary medicine and plant fertilisation.

Plant Fertilization

The versatility of the molecules under consideration was recognised, and an experiment was conducted in which they were applied to plants. Consequently, this initiative has led to the establishment of a distinct and substantial agricultural division.

The primary objectives of this initiative were twofold: firstly, to enhance crop yields, and secondly, to ensure the protection of plants. Research into growth stimulants has demonstrated an impact on the synchronous and uniform flowering of plants, which in turn has a direct effect on the quality and quantity of the harvest. Research has demonstrated that these stimulants modulate the levels of phytohormones such as gibberellins and auxins.

The protective properties of the aforementioned molecules have been demonstrated to enable plants to combat abiotic and biotic stress. For instance, resistance to moisture deficiency, temperature fluctuations, oxidative stress increases, and the process of stabilising cell membranes has been observed. In addition, experimental tests have demonstrated resistance to viral diseases and bacterial infections.

Human Health

Pharmacology represents a pivotal area of research, with studies indicating the efficacy and safety of low-molecular bonds as a therapeutic alternative for various serious diseases. 

  These molecules function as signalling molecules, capable of binding to the genes without altering them, thereby activating the body's intrinsic mechanisms, including the immune system and potentially addressing oncological pathologies.

The minute size of these molecules enables them to traverse the blood-brain barrier, thereby facilitating direct interaction with the brain. This characteristic renders them well-suited for the treatment of neurodegenerative diseases, including Parkinson's, Huntington's and Alzheimer's. 

The universality of these bonds also allows for their application in the context of retina-related diseases, such as dry macular degeneration.

Veterinary Medicine

The efficacy of low-molecular-weight bonds in facilitating cellular processes has led to notable advancements in the development of therapeutic medications for farm and companion animals.

Current research has demonstrated the efficacy of research in the field of musculoskeletal systems and endometritis in bovines, as well as gastrointestinal diseases and urolithiasis in canines.

Furthermore, the potential exists for the study of the effects of molecules on growth, meat composition and immunity in trout.

The development of this field is ongoing, with plans to create drugs for the immune and other systems in animals that are of key importance in the livestock sector.
